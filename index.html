Effects of Innate Savagery in Human Nature

Intro:

Are humans inherently inclined towards violence and destruction? Deep within us lies an innate urge within, a remnant of our primitive ancestry, waiting to be awakened. Innate savagery is the raw, untamed aspect of humanity, often concealed beneath a veil of civilization, social norms, and morality. It represents the instinctual drive for survival, an urge for dominance, and a capacity for violence that lurks within all of us, ready to pounce in times of crisis or chaos. The exploration of the effects of innate savagery on our actions reveal the importance of the social norms that keep humanity civilized. While the concept of innate savagery suggests that humans have a tendency to make morally unethical decisions, our individual choices and backgrounds also significantly influence our behavior. This nuanced relationship between social norms and primal instincts illustrates the complexity of human nature.
